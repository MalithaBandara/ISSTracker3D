RUSSIAN MODULES

Zarya (FGB)

  • Launch date - 1998-11-20
  • Assembly flight - 1A/R
  • Launch vehicle - Proton-K
  • Length - 12.56 m (41.2 ft)
  • Diameter - 4.1 m (13 ft)
  • Mass - 24,968 kg (55,045 lb)
  • Description - The Zarya Module, also known by the technical term Functional Cargo Block and the Russian acronym FGB, was the first component launched for the International Space Station.

Zvezda (Service Module)

  • Launch date - 2000-07-12
  • Assembly flight - 1R
  • Launch vehicle - Proton-K
  • Length - 13.1 m (43 ft)
  • Diameter - 4.2 m (14 ft)
  • Mass - 24,604 kg (54,243 lb)
  • Description - The Zvezda Service Module was the first fully Russian contribution to the International Space Station and served as the early cornerstone for the first human habitation of the station. The module provides station living quarters, life support systems, electrical power distribution, data processing systems, flight control systems and propulsion systems.

Poisk (MRM-2)

  • Launch date - 2009-11-10
  • Assembly flight - 5R
  • Launch vehicle - "Soyuz-U (Progress M-MIM2)"
  • Length - 16 ft
  • Diameter - 8.4 ft
  • Mass - 8,367 lb
  • Description - Poisk, also known as the Mini-Research Module 2 (MRM2), provides a port for the docking of Soyuz crew ships.

Rassvet (MRM-1)

  • Launch date - 2010-05-14
  • Assembly flight - ULF4
  • Launch vehicle - Space Shuttle Atlantis (STS-132)
  • Length - 19.7 ft
  • Diameter - 7.7 ft
  • Mass - 11,188 lb
  • Description - Rassvet is primarily used for cargo storage and as a docking port for visiting spacecraft.

Nauka (MLM-U)

  • Launch date - 2021-07-21
  • Assembly flight - 3R
  • Launch vehicle - Proton-M
  • Length - 13 m (43 ft)
  • Diameter - 4.25 m (13.9 ft)
  • Mass - 20,300 kg (44,800 lb)
  • Description - Nauka will serve as a new science facility, docking port, and spacewalk airlock for future operations.

Prichal

  • Launch date - 2021-11-24
  • Assembly flight - 6R
  • Launch vehicle - Soyuz 2.1b(Progress M-UM)
  • Length - 4.91 m (16.1 ft)
  • Diameter - 3.3 m (11 ft)
  • Mass - 3,890 kg (8,580 lb)
  • Description - Prichal, also known as Uzlovoy Module or UM is a 4-ton ball-shaped module that will provide the Russian segment additional docking ports to receive Soyuz MS and Progress MS spacecraft.

JAPANESE MODULES

Kibo pressurized module

  • Launch date - 2008-05-31
  • Launch vehicle - Space Shuttle Endeavour (STS-124)
  • Length - 36.7 ft
  • Diameter - 14.4 ft
  • Mass - 35,050 lb
  • Description - The Pressurized Module (PM) is the core component connected to the port hatch of Harmony. It is cylindrical in shape and contains twenty-three International Standard Payload Racks (ISPRs), ten of which are dedicated to science experiments while the remaining thirteen are dedicated to Kibō's systems and storage.

  • Launch vehicle - Space Shuttle Atlantis (STS-122)
  • Length - 7 m(23 ft)
  • Diameter - 4.5 m(15 ft)
  • Mass - 12,800 kg (28,219 lb)
